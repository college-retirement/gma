Junior Year

  • Visit college fairs, talk to friends and alumni; look at various college guides and ask counselors and teachers for suggestions.
  • Write for catalogs and see what the colleges say they want.
  • Take the PSAT/NMSQT; take SAT II's and Advanced Placement Examinations at the end of appropriate courses of study.
  • Meet with your school counselor and, with your parents, develop a list of colleges of interest.
  • Consider visiting colleges in the spring or over the summer.
  • Mention your plans to the teachers you may want to have write your recommendations.
  • Take the SATs in the spring.
  • Keep a journal or collect interesting "important moment" articles from your reading as samples for your essay.
  • Schedule a strong senior year program.


Senior Year

SEPTEMBER
  • Focus on your classes and achieve the best grades possible in your senior program.
  • Attend college conferences at your high school.
  • Set up campus visits and interviews; attend prospective student days and open houses at colleges of interest.
  • Line up your recommending teachers.
  • Request application packets from colleges that interest you.


OCTOBER
  • Confirm your choices with your counselor.
  • Give recommending teachers the appropriate letters and envelopes. You may want to give them a short resume. You will definitely want to drop them a thank-you note after the deadlines.
  • Take the SAT I and/or the SAT II's.
  • Pool application essay questions by type; begin thinking about these questions.
  • Research scholarships that may apply to you; get FAFSA aid forms from your guidance counselor.
  • If applying Early Decision, start writing your application and essay.


NOVEMBER
  • Early Decision deadlines are often November 1 or 15.
  • If applying Regular Decision, begin filling out applications and writing essays. Your deadlines will fall between December 1 and February 1.
  • You may take the SAT I and/or SAT II's again.


DECEMBER
  • Finish essays and copy them or attach them to your applications. You can download applications from various software (Common Application, ExPAN, College Link) or use the forms the colleges provide.
  • Proofread everything TWICE! Make a checklist to be sure you've had scores sent, enclosed checks, and notified your counselor about each school you're applying to. Mail applications early.


JANUARY-APRIL
  • Follow-up on any missing details.
  • Continue to focus on producing a solid senior record.
  • Continue to visit or interview if you missed a school of interest; it's best to see a college when classes are in session.
  • Prepare and file financial aid forms (FAFSA).

Source: "The College Application Essay," Sarah Myers McGinty, The College Board, 2001.
