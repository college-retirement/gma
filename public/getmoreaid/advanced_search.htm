What does selectivity mean for me?

Selectivity doesn’t have anything to do with the quality of the education you’ll receive. It’s simply about the number of students that apply to a college and how many of them get in. You probably hear a lot about very selective colleges that admit just a small percent of applicants, but those colleges are in the minority. In fact, more than 1,600 colleges admit almost all high school grads who apply. Top five things you should know about college admissions.

Most U.S. colleges are coed.

All public colleges and most private colleges are coed, meaning that both men and women attend. There are only about 60 colleges specifically for men and about 50 specifically for women. Find out more about types of colleges.

Live on campus or commute?

At residential colleges, most students live on campus, often in dorms. At commuter colleges, most students drive to campus for classes and activities, but return home at the end of the day. Get advice about making the most of college life as a commuter.
